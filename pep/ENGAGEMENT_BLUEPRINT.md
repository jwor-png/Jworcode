# Engagement Blueprint

The standard shape of every engagement record, inbound or outbound. Same structure, different register: inbound is warm, outbound is cold.

## Sections, in order
1. **The lead / capture** — who, their business, the context, how it came in.
2. **Your read** — a slot for your own instinct on the person and the opportunity.
3. **Investigative intelligence** — the person, the firm, sourced.
4. **Five angles** (the rule below).
5. **Pre-call plan** — posture, roles, the ten questions, the give, the offering ladder, next steps.
6. **Acceleration Field read** — the three variables scored, the tier, Signer or Circler.
7. **Confidence and sources.**

## The rules

### Five angles
Exactly five. **Three personalised**, drawn only from verified intelligence, things you are certain on, never guesses. **Two industry-type**, sized to that type and size of business, never scaled up to a global giant they are not.

### On-call intelligence: the ten questions (ask on every call)
You explain yourself well and extract poorly. These pull current state, propensity, constraints, hunger, and whether they are a Signer or a Circler.
1. What has prompted you to look at this now?
2. Where are you using AI today, and how is that going?
3. What would have to be true in twelve months for this to have been worth it?
4. Who else needs to be on board for something to happen?
5. Have you brought outside help in on something like this before, and how did it go?
6. What is the cost of doing nothing for another year?
7. When you have moved on something important before, how fast did it go from decision to action?
8. What is the one thing that, if AI handled it, would change your week?
9. Is this about your own organisation, your clients, or both?
10. If we showed you something that worked, what would you want to do next?

### The give: one or two demos, no more
A call is forty minutes. Bring one or two demos, about five minutes each, built to land. No more, because you still have to ask the ten, build rapport and move to agreement.

### Confidence discipline
Every record separates verified (sourced), analysis (a hypothesis to test in the room), and gaps. Stand over the sourced facts, hold the analysis lightly.
