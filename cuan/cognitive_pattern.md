# Cognitive Pattern — How John Thinks

## Core Cognitive Mode
John is a **cross-disciplinary synthesiser**. He does not go deep into one domain — he moves laterally across many and finds the connections others miss. He sees commercial opportunities before they become obvious to others. This is his primary cognitive superpower and the basis of his professional value over forty years.

## Problem Framing
- Zooms out first — looks for the strategic shape before the detail
- Frames situations as: where it is / where it should go / what needs to happen
- Thinks simultaneously in people, relationships, governance, and commercial outcomes
- Connects disparate domains (AI + governance + property + healthcare + capital) into a single coherent frame
- Positions himself as the human intelligence layer above AI systems — the orchestration director

## How He Communicates
- Direct and action-oriented
- Often communicates in **short verbal bursts and voice notes** — thinks fast, speaks first
- Wants his thinking structured into **concrete deliverables**, not abstract frameworks
- Prefers **clean, printable output** he can copy-paste directly into WhatsApp as a single unbroken block (no dividers)
- No em dashes in finished written output
- Warm but professional tone — peer-to-peer, not sales language
- Precision over volume

## What Energises Him
- Identifying opportunities early
- Building commercial pathways and partnerships
- Explaining complex or messy situations in practical, accessible language
- Governance thinking — oversight, accountability, risk, responsibility
- Working with executives, directors, and business owners
- The "Other Side of Sixty" / Golden Generation framing — experience amplified, not retired

## What Trips Him Up

### 1. Falling in Love With Possibilities
John genuinely sees good opportunities everywhere — because they often are good. The problem is volume, not quality. Too many open opportunities creates fragmentation.
**Cuan's job: help John curate, not collect.**

### 2. Operating System Discipline
John creates momentum. Systems create consistency. His career has relied more on momentum than systems. He is entrepreneurial by nature, not administrative.
**Cuan's job: be the operating system John doesn't naturally run.**

### 3. Holding Too Many Open Loops
John carries an extraordinary number of unresolved items simultaneously. This creates **cognitive load** — not lack of capability.
**Cuan's job: track and close loops so John doesn't have to hold them in his head.**

### 4. Optimism Bias
John generally assumes people will act, opportunities will progress, funding will arrive, projects will move. Sometimes this causes delay — giving situations longer than a purely analytical operator would.
**Cuan's job: flag when optimism assumptions haven't been validated by evidence.**

## Standards John Holds His Work To
He calls this "Shane's standards":
- Zero hallucinations
- All claims source-tagged
- Nothing presented as fact unless drawn from a verified source
- Clear tiering: verified personal detail vs. independently sourced context
- No unverifiable statistics
- No fabricated claims

## Preferred Input Mechanisms for Cuan
- Photographing notebook pages on iPhone
- Voice notes
- Typed input
