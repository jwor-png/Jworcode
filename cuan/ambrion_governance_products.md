## Document 1: Governance of AI — Board Training Deck
**File:** Governance of AI - Ambrion.pdf
**Author:** Pat McGrath, Founding Partner, Ambrion AI — April 2026
**Format:** 8-slide presentation, Section 17 of a Corporate Governance Training series
**Title:** The Board and Artificial Intelligence

### Core Premise
The role of the board and directors in a world where AI is now regulated, and accountability sits with people, not systems.

### Why Now — The Regulatory Wall
Three jurisdictions, arriving simultaneously:

EU: EU AI Act (Reg. 2024/1689, phased 2026–2027); Digital Omnibus / DORA / NIS2; ISO/IEC 42001 (certifiable AI management standard)

Ireland: Regulation of AI Bill 2026 (enforceable obligations); National AI Office (to be established August 2026, 15 competent authorities nominated); Digital Ireland and AI for Care strategies

UK: FCA Principles approach — named accountable senior managers under SMCR; Operational Resilience — weak signal scanning, drift, incident reconstruction; Consumer Duty — protect against foreseeable and predictive harm and bias

### EU AI Act — Four Risk Tiers
1. Unacceptable (BANNED): Social scoring, biometric identification, emotion inference, untargeted facial image scraping, harm to vulnerable people
2. High risk (HIGHLY REGULATED): AI in regulated products and safety-critical uses. Conformity, documentation, human oversight required
3. Limited risk (TRANSPARENCY OBLIGATIONS): Chatbots and deepfakes — people must be told they are dealing with AI or synthetic content
4. Minimal risk (LARGELY UNREGULATED): Majority of AI uses. Voluntary good practice applies

### Five Director Duties
1. Systems, policies and governance — procedures in place to comply with EU AI Act and Digital Services Act
2. Appoint an Accountable Executive — one named individual owns AI and DSA obligations; diffuse responsibility is a legal risk
3. Complete an AI inventory and risk classification — know every AI system the organisation runs and where it sits under the Act
4. Review the board's AI competency — assess the board's own understanding and training needs
5. A quarterly board dashboard — regular, meaningful reporting on AI activities and risks the board can actually challenge

### The Ambrion Model — AI Governing AI
A swarm of 12 agents carries the governance load so the people who are accountable can spend their judgement where it counts.

- AUTOMATION handles volume: continuous monitoring, mapping and logging at a scale no team can match by hand
- HUMANS hold judgement: interpretation, risk acceptance and accountability stay with people
- THE BOARD holds liability: formal sign-off cannot be automated; the board carries it

### The 12-Agent Architecture — Three Layers
Layer 1 — Intelligence (Do we know our obligations): 5 agents
- Obligation Interpreter
- Obligation Mapper
- Risk Forecaster
- Weak Signal Scanner
- Regulator Sync Engine

Layer 2 — Control (Are we operationally compliant): 3 agents
- Governance Workflow Controller
- Role and Liability Allocator
- Policy Translator

Layer 3 — Assurance and Defensibility (Can we prove it): 4 agents
- Evidence Extractor
- Provenance Architect
- Board Pack Generator
- Attestation and Submission Writer

### Five Human Gates — Non-Negotiable
These five approvals cannot be automated:
1. Legal interpretation approval — is our reading of the obligation correct (INTELLIGENCE layer)
2. Automated control design approval — are the controls fit for the risk (CONTROL)
3. Risk acceptance approval — do we accept this residual risk (CONTROL)
4. Deployment approval — is this system cleared to go live (CONTROL)
5. Regulatory attestation approval — do we formally certify to the regulator (ASSURANCE)

### Closing Frame
"Automation handles execution. Humans hold judgement and liability."

## Document 2: Board Governance Platform — 2-Pager Capability Overview
**File:** BOARD GOVERNANCE PLATFORM copy.docx

### What It Is
An AI-powered board governance and compliance platform. Six capabilities:

| # | Capability | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Policy and Document Intelligence | Digitises and indexes existing policy library; AI identifies gaps, flags outdated content, connects related policies |
| 2 | Regulatory Monitoring | Monitors Irish, European, and international legislation; identifies which policies are affected when regulations change |
| 3 | Company Secretary Alerts | Automated, prioritised alerts with specific policy impact, recommended response, and clear timeline |
| 4 | Board Paper Preparation | AI-supported drafting, version control, and secure distribution of board and committee papers |
| 5 | Meeting Intelligence | Automatic extraction of action items from minutes; each action assigned, tracked, and reported |
| 6 | Secure Board Portal | Dedicated, role-based secure portal for directors and authorised staff; all activity logged for audit |

### Approach
Not a fixed software product. Discovery engagement first. Scope and build in phases, beginning with highest immediate value. Platform built on secure, modern AI infrastructure. Data stays under client control. Designed for EU AI Act, GDPR, sectoral codes, and charity governance requirements.
