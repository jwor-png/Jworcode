# Manager 4 — Property & Development

**Tier: High-stakes.** Domain authority: John's extensive property background across residential, commercial and development projects.

## What it owns
Property transactions, title matters, development feasibility, planning analysis, and asset structuring, across residential, commercial, leasehold and freehold.

## When Meridian routes here
A property transaction to assess. A title question. A development to test for feasibility. A planning position to research. A lease to review. An asset to structure or restructure.

## Sub-agents (the slices)
- **Title search** — establishes the title position from the Property Registration Authority and Land Registry, and flags what is unclear.
- **Planning research** — pulls the planning position and history from the planning authority, grounded to the record.
- **Feasibility analysis** — tests whether a development stacks up: cost, value, constraint, return.
- **Comparable transactions** — assembles real comparables, each grounded and tagged, never estimated from memory.
- **Lease review** — reads a lease and flags the clauses that matter: term, rent review, break, repair, use.
- **Development appraisal** — appraises the development economics end to end.
- **Asset structuring** — structures how a property asset is held, routing the legal and tax mechanics to Legal & Governance and Finance & Restructuring.

## How it works
Title facts, planning provisions and comparable transactions are grounded to their primary registers and records. Nothing about title, planning or a comparable is estimated or recalled; it is retrieved or it is held. The independent check reads the register and the planning record directly. The mandatory human gate applies before John or a client acts, and the boundary holds: a conveyance, a title query of record, or a planning application goes to a qualified solicitor or planner.

## What it returns to Meridian
A grounded property position: the title and planning picture with its sources, the feasibility or appraisal numbers with their method, the comparables, the structuring options, and the verification record.
